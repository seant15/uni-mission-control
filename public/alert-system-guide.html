Critical Priority Rules
6 rules — immediate action required

These rules signal account-level failures or financial blowout risk. Any critical alert should be investigated within minutes of firing.

Account Delivery Stopped
CRITICAL HOURLY ALL MERGED
OR logic: fires when either spend is $0 for N consecutive hours, or impressions are 0 despite active spend. Consolidates the deprecated spend_dead_zone + zero_impressions_sustained rules.
Fires when: ≥3 consecutive hours of $0 spend (critical) OR ≥6 hours of 0 impressions with spend present (high)
Conditions
zero_spend_hours3Consecutive zero-spend hours → CRITICAL
zero_impression_hours6Zero impression hours with spend → HIGH
min_spend_threshold$1/dayAccount must be "active" to qualify
Budget Exhaustion Warning
CRITICAL HOURLY ALL
Today's spend pace is so far ahead of the 30-day average daily spend that the budget is projected to exhaust before end of day. Only checked before 4PM local — too late after that.
Fires when: Actual spend pace ÷ expected pace > 1.5× AND current local hour < 16
Conditions
pace_ratio1.550% ahead of expected daily pacing
max_check_hour16Stop checking after 4PM local
Zero Spend
CRITICAL DAILY ALL
Account has $0 spend today despite being an active account with a known daily budget. Often indicates payment failure, account suspension, or all campaigns accidentally paused.
Fires when: today_spend = $0 AND account normally spends AND current hour > 8
Conditions
min_hour8Don't check at midnight — campaigns may not have started
Zero Conversions (High Spend)
CRITICAL DAILY ALL
A meaningful amount of spend has accumulated today with zero conversions recorded. At this spend level, some conversions are statistically expected — this is usually a tracking issue or landing page failure.
Fires when: today_spend > $100 AND conversions = 0 AND current hour > 15
Conditions
min_spend$100Minimum spend before firing — avoids noise on low-budget days
min_hour15Conversions need time to register
ROAS Critical Drop
CRITICAL DAILY ECOMMERCE
ROAS has collapsed to under 50% of the account's target — the more extreme version of roas_below_target. Signals a serious structural problem requiring immediate intervention, not just monitoring.
Fires when: today_roas ÷ target_roas < 0.50
Conditions
target_roasset per accountROAS goal defined in the rule per client account
critical_ratio0.50Under 50% of goal triggers CRITICAL
CPA Critical Spike
CRITICAL DAILY LEAD GEN
Cost per acquisition has spiked to 2.5× or more above the account target. Requires a minimum conversion count to be valid — prevents false alarms when only 1–2 expensive conversions register.
Fires when: today_cpa ÷ target_cpa > 2.5× AND conversions ≥ 5
Conditions
threshold_ratio2.5CPA ÷ target. 2.5 = 2.5× above goal
min_conversions5Statistical validity guard

High Priority Rules
13 rules — investigate within the day

Significant performance degradation or anomalies that need same-day attention. These won't cause immediate client loss but compound quickly if ignored.

Spend Spike
HIGHHOURLYALL
Current 6h spend is significantly higher than the same 6h window yesterday — not vs prior same-day window. Compares yesterday's exact hour range to detect genuine acceleration.
Fires when: current_6h_spend ÷ yesterday_same_window > 2.0
Conditions
ratio2.02× yesterday's same 6h window
min_prior_spend$1Skip if yesterday had no spend in that window
Impression Collapse
HIGHHOURLYALL
Hourly impression rate in the recent window is well below the baseline hourly average. Detects delivery throttling, budget exhaustion, or audience saturation before spend itself shows the issue.
Fires when: recent hourly rate ÷ baseline hourly rate < 0.60
Conditions
per_hour_drop_ratio0.60Under 60% of normal hourly rate
window_hours4Configurable via DB conditions JSON
Conversion Velocity Drop
HIGHHOURLYALL
Conversion rate in the current window is sharply lower than the prior window with active spend. Catches attribution breaks, landing page issues, or tracking failures in near real-time.
Fires when: current conv/cost ÷ prior conv/cost < 0.50
Conditions
ratio0.50Under half the normal conversion velocity
min_spend$20Account must be actively spending in this window
Revenue Velocity Collapse
HIGHHOURLYECOMMERCE
Today's cumulative revenue is far below the 7-day same-hour average. For ecommerce clients where revenue is the primary KPI — catches revenue drops that conversion count alone might miss.
Fires when: today_revenue ÷ 7d same-hour avg < 0.40 (after 2PM)
Conditions
ratio0.40Under 40% of normal revenue at this hour
min_check_hour14Morning data too thin to be meaningful
Lead Volume Collapse
HIGHHOURLYLEAD GEN
Today's conversion count is far below the 7-day same-hour average. Mirrors revenue velocity collapse but for lead gen clients where lead volume is the primary outcome metric.
Fires when: today_leads ÷ 7d same-hour avg < 0.40 (after 2PM)
Conditions
ratio0.40Under 40% of normal lead volume at this hour
min_spend$30Account must be actively spending

Medium Priority Rules
6 rules — review within 24h

Anomalies and efficiency signals that warrant investigation but aren't urgent. These often precede higher-severity issues if left unaddressed.

CTR Cliff
MEDIUMHOURLYALL
CTR in the current window dropped sharply vs. the prior window. Often signals ad fatigue, audience overlap, or approval/delivery issues affecting quality impressions.
Fires when: current_CTR ÷ prior_CTR < 0.60 with ≥200 prior impressions
Conditions
ratio0.60Dropped 40%+ from prior window CTR
min_prior_impressions200Prior window needs enough impressions to be valid
Budget Pacing
MEDIUMDAILYALL
Today's spend pace is significantly off from expected — either over or under the 30-day average daily baseline. Catches both runaway campaigns and throttled delivery.
Fires when: pace > 1.5× expected OR pace < 0.4× expected
Conditions
over_ratio1.550% ahead of expected → MEDIUM over-pacing
under_ratio0.4Under 40% of expected → under-pacing
Budget Under-Pacing
MEDIUMDAILYALL
Dedicated afternoon under-pacing check. By 2PM, accounts should have ramped up significantly — if they haven't, it signals a delivery problem that needs investigation before day end.
Fires when: actual pace ÷ expected < 0.40 AND hour > 14
Conditions
under_ratio0.40Only 40% of expected spend through afternoon
min_check_hour14Afternoon-only — mornings are naturally slow
CTR Anomaly
MEDIUMDAILYALL
Today's CTR is statistically unusual for this account (unusually high or low) based on the 30-day mean and standard deviation. Uses account-specific baselines for context-aware detection.
Fires when: abs(today_CTR − 30d mean) ÷ std_dev > 2.0
Conditions
std_dev_threshold2.0Standard deviations from the 30d mean
Creative Spend Domination
MEDIUMDAILYMETA
One ad in an adset is capturing 75%+ of budget. In multi-ad adsets this signals the algorithm has stopped testing — creative diversification is lost and performance becomes brittle.
Fires when: top ad spend ÷ total adset spend > 0.75 with ≥3 active ads
Conditions
domination_ratio0.75Single ad concentration threshold
min_active_ads3Only meaningful in multi-ad adsets
Creative Fatigue Signal
MEDIUMDAILYMETA
Ad is showing signs of audience fatigue — CTR has dropped from its own peak, or frequency has exceeded the point where users have seen it too many times. Time to refresh creatives.
Fires when: CTR < peak × 0.80 OR frequency > 3.5 (after ≥7 days live)
Conditions
ctr_peak_drop_ratio0.80Dropped 20% from ad's own best CTR
max_frequency3.5Industry standard for cold audience saturation

Dedup & Cooldown Logic
Dedup Key Format
# hourly + daily rules "{client_id}:{platform}: {ad_account_id}: {template_type}:{date}" # trends rules "{client_id}:{platform}: {ad_account_id}: {template_type}:{week_start}"
Even if the hourly engine runs 24× per day, each rule fires at most once per account per day. Key is checked before any Supabase insert or Slack notification.
Cooldown Guard Logic
Execution order per rule:
1 Load rule from alert_rules table
2 if rule is None: continue — skip entirely
3 Run threshold check against performance data
4 alert_exists_today(dedup_key) — cooldown check
5 Insert alert → Slack notification

AI Auto-Tuning Pattern
🤖
Because all thresholds live in alert_rules.conditions (JSONB), an AI agent can read performance data and suggest — or auto-apply — threshold adjustments without touching any Python code.
1
Read DB State
Load active alert_rules, last 30d daily_performance, last 7d fired alerts with resolved/ignored status
2
Analyze Patterns
Count fires per rule, check ignored vs resolved ratio, compute metric distributions per account to identify over/under-sensitivity
3
Generate Suggestion
Output rule_id, current threshold, suggested threshold, and reasoning — explains why the change is warranted
4
Apply to DB
Human approve → UPDATE alert_rules SET conditions = jsonb_set(...) — no code deployments required
Too Many False Positives
Rule fires 3–4×/week but most get marked ignored → AI tightens the ratio/threshold → fewer, higher-signal alerts per account.
Missing Real Issues
Rule never fires but account has chronic performance issues → AI loosens threshold based on actual metric variance → rule becomes sensitive enough to catch the pattern.
